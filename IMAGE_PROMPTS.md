## Continuity Notes for 10⁻⁴ to 10⁴

These nine images form a continuous zoom sequence. **Every image uses a top-down perspective — the camera is always looking straight down from above, centered on the man's fingertip.** When generating them:

1. **Start with 10⁰ (10+00-person.jpg)** — this is the anchor image. Generate it first. The camera looks straight down at two people lying on a blanket; the man's fingertip on a book page is the exact center of the frame.
2. **Zoom out**: Generate 10¹, 10², 10³, 10⁴ by progressively pulling the camera straight up. Each image should contain the previous image's content at its center, now smaller. The perspective remains perfectly top-down.
3. **Zoom in**: Generate 10⁻¹, 10⁻², 10⁻³, 10⁻⁴ by progressively descending straight down toward the man's fingertip. Each image is a closer crop of the previous, always centered on the same point.
4. Use the same lighting, color temperature, and time of day across all nine.
5. The checkered blanket pattern should be a recognizable anchor at every scale where it's visible.
6. **Critical**: The center point of every image in this sequence is the same physical location — the man's fingertip on the book page.
